A first dispatch.

I lit the candle three times last night. The third match took. By then I had abandoned the chapter I was reading on Beowulf and was instead looking at the ceiling — which is, admittedly, where most reading ends in October. The dispatch begins with a small confession: this is not a productivity newsletter.

## The card

**The Hermit.** Lantern raised, alone on the mountain.

The Hermit appears when a week wants to be private. He carries his own light, walks ahead of the path he is making, and tells nobody where he is going. Take him as permission to study what you are studying without telling anyone what you are studying. A small society of one. The hermit is not lonely — he is *withdrawn*, which is the more useful word, because it implies a deliberate motion away from somewhere noisier.

A practical use: if you have a project this week that you have been describing to people more than working on, stop describing it. Withdraw.

(The hermit, of course, would tell me not to write this dispatch.)

## A poem for the season

> *October's a lyric long since taken;*
> *the candles know what month it is.*
> *We read, and the leaves do their own work,*
> *and the page does not get any longer*
> *no matter how late we keep at it.*

— from an unfinished thing I have been adding to since August. The point of an unfinished thing is that it stays company.
