It is raining in the fictional college and the radiators are doing the medieval thing where they whistle. The desk lamp is yellow. The cat is on the manuscript. None of these are problems.

## The card

**The High Priestess.** Seated between two pillars; the moon at her feet; a small scroll, half-hidden, on her lap.

The High Priestess follows the Hermit very deliberately. The Hermit withdrew to *make* knowledge; the High Priestess sits with knowledge already in her possession and decides what to do with it. She is not a teacher. She does not explain. The scroll on her lap — by tradition called *Tora* — is half-rolled and partly covered by a sleeve. The signal is: *some things, you keep*.

A practical use this week: if someone asks you for an opinion you have not yet finished forming, you are allowed to not give it. "I am not done thinking" is a complete sentence and the High Priestess would approve.

## A book I have read three times this year

**Susanna Clarke — *Piranesi***.

It is a short book. A man lives in a house that is also an ocean and also an archive. He labels his statues and keeps weather notes. Something is wrong, and he is the last to know.

I cannot tell you more without ruining it. It is about loneliness, scholarship, and the small consolations of cataloguing. I think you should read it slowly, on a Sunday, with the candle on. Then read it again three months later.

## Small things from the inbox

A reader writes: *should I keep a tarot journal?*

Yes — but only if you would also keep a regular journal anyway. If you would not, the tarot journal becomes a chore you bought a moleskine for. Better to write the card on a Post-it, stick it inside the front of whatever book you are reading this week, and see if it speaks to you. (Or doesn't.)
